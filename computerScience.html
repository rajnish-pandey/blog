Here are 5 reasons to fall in love with computer science careers. Keep reading to learn more.

A career in computer science is a career you can fall in love with. There are so many reasons to fall in love with computer science careers. Here are our top 5 favorite reasons computer science careers are the best!

1. Endless Job Opportunities

As technology advances, so does the need for computer scientists. There are so many options for computer science careers. Here’s just a taste of some jobs that are available to those knowledgeable in computer science.

Software Engineer

A software engineer is the one that creates and manages computer programs including customer relations software, computer games, operations systems, and even video editing software. Software engineers work with the clients to plan and design the software for the target audience.

Computer Programmer

Computer programmers are the ones that write the code for the program that the software engineer wants to create. A computer programmer uses their creativity and problem-solving skills to figure out the best code for the software or application to give the user the best experience. Troubleshooting and fixing bugs are also part of the job description.

Mobile App Developer

Mobile app developers and computer programmers are similar, but also quite different. They both use code to write their programs, they both have to be creative and good problem solvers. A mobile app developer needs to also be proficient in the mobile app platforms, Android and iOS. Mobile app developers could also be part of the planning meetings to ensure that they understand what the target audience is looking for.

Web Developer

Web developers build websites. A graphic designer gives the design to the web developer, who then turns that into code that computers and various devices can read. Images and videos also get turned into code by a web developer for various devices to understand. Before building the website it is important for the web developer to understand the needs of the client and the target audience as well. Along with the interface and user experience specialist, they work together to ensure that the flow of the website makes sense for the needs of the client.

Database Administrator

Database administrators collect the data that the website and mobile apps collect and make sense of it all and then tell the client about the data. This data that is collected can contain sensitive information, so it is also the job of a database administrator to keep the information safe. Database administrators keep information secure through backups and regularly scheduled maintenance checks.

2. The pay is high

According to PayScale, the average annual pay range for those holding a bachelor’s degree in computer science is $58,000-$119,000. As you can see from above, average yearly salaries range depending on the computer science career you choose to go with, but still are usually on the higher side. The average entry level salary for a computer scientists is $69,789. As your career progresses, so does your salary — to an average of $91,415 at mid-career level, $101,067 at the experienced level, and $118,359 at the late career level.

3. Job satisfaction

A study done in the UK where they found that “the job satisfaction in IT is double that of the national average for other work sectors.” That says that those with a career in computer science really enjoy the work they do and the environment they work in. Computer scientists get to put their passions into their work and really find a way to help society with new technology.

4. Computer science is cross-cultural

A career in computer science can be applied all over the world. As a computer scientist you have the opportunity to change the lives of people everywhere since technology is a major part of most people’s lives. Not only can you change the lives of those around the world, but it also means that you can work anywhere in the world. Whether its for an international company or working remote, it is possible to work from various places around the globe. Take advantage of the flexibility and travel the world while you work. You never know who you might meet and connect with. Taking online courses with University of the People allows you to connect with people from around the world. Use the ability to work from anywhere you want to meet the people you were in classes with

5. Freelance while you study

Learn what it’s like to have a computer science career as you are learning the skills. Take what you learn in the classroom and apply it in real life to real situations, and make a little money while you do it. Freelancing is a great way to test your skills and apply classroom knowledge to the real world. You are able to jump right in and apply the skills learned in the classroom directly to real situations since these skills are in such high demand.
